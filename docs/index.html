Quality Score Methodology

Each ad is scored out of 100 by AI analysis (GPT-4.1) across 6 categories weighted for aged care advertising effectiveness.

CategoryWeightWhat It Measures
Copy Quality25 ptsClarity, persuasion, emotional resonance, grammar, CTA strength. For aged care: empathy, dignity, trust signals.
Visual Quality20 ptsDesign professionalism, brand alignment, image quality and relevance to aged care.
Targeting Signals15 ptsDoes the ad speak to the right audience? Older Australians, adult children making decisions, carers.
Compliance15 ptsAged care advertising regulations, no misleading claims, ACQSC alignment, appropriate imagery.
Brand Consistency15 ptsAlignment with brand voice — for Trilogy: self-managed, empowerment, independence, transparent fees.
Freshness10 ptsIs the ad current and timely? References to Support at Home, recent policy changes.

Trilogy Strengths

+ Clear value proposition around maximizing care hours and funding efficiency
+ Consistent branding across platforms

Gaps vs Competitors

- Limited creative diversity (mostly static images on Google)
- Narrow CTA focus compared to competitors
- Messaging is highly functional, lacking emotional/lifestyle appeal
- Potential gaps in targeting broader family decision-makers
